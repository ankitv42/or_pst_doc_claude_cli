  ---
  What's still keeping it from 9+

  Three things a senior Google engineer would still probe:

  1. No retry/fallback on Groq (single point of failure)
  If Groq is down, every pipeline fails. No exponential backoff, no secondary provider. "What's your availability story?" — you can't answer this well yet.

  2. SqliteSaver is single-instance only
  Better than MemorySaver, but still breaks in a multi-worker or multi-pod deployment. Render runs one instance so it's fine for now, but you can't claim horizontal
  scalability.

  3. No operational metrics
  LangSmith is observability for debugging. You still have zero SLA metrics — ESCALATE rate over time, avg pipeline latency, HITL approval time, cost per decision.
  Google specifically asks "how would you monitor this in production?"
